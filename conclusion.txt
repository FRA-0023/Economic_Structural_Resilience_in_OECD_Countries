\section{Conclusion}
% =========================================================

This project developed a POSet-based framework for comparing economic structural resilience across OECD countries without reducing multidimensional configurations into a single weighted index. By using Partial Order Theory, the analysis preserves the multidimensional nature of resilience and explicitly recognizes cases of structural incomparability between countries.

The empirical POSet results show that OECD economies cannot be fully represented through a simple linear ranking. Instead, countries form distinct structural profiles based on different combinations of innovation capacity, institutional robustness, fiscal space, energy independence, and human capital. The presence of incomparable profiles is one of the main findings of the project, as it demonstrates that multiple economic configurations may coexist without one being objectively superior across all dimensions.

These findings support the idea that long-term structural conditions influence the capacity of economies to absorb and recover from major shocks.

At the same time, the analysis confirms that economic resilience cannot be explained by a single variable or by a purely compensatory index. Some countries perform strongly in certain dimensions while remaining vulnerable in others, which explains why incomparability is not a methodological weakness but an important analytical result. The POSet framework therefore provides a more transparent and interpretable way to study resilience than traditional composite rankings.

Overall, the project shows that Partial Order Theory can serve as a useful methodological tool for economic resilience analysis. It allows structural strengths, vulnerabilities, and alternative development paths to be represented simultaneously, while keeping the validation stage separate from the construction of the ordering itself. The framework can be extended in future work through dynamic POSet evolution, additional shock episodes, and broader structural indicators.
